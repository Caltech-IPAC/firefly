IRSA/WISE Image Service: Overview

The IRSA/WISE Image Service is the final repository for all of the data collected by WISE. The most detailed description about the acquisition, processing, and calibration of WISE data can be found in the Explanatory Supplements for the All-Sky Data Release , 3-Band Cryo Data Release, Post-Cryo Preliminary Data Release, and Preliminary Data Release Products, respectively.

The Spacecraft and Instruments

The Wide-Field Infrared Survey Explorer was launched on a Delta II rocket from Vandenberg Air Force Base into a sun-synchronous orbit on December 14, 2009. Consisting of a 0.4-meter telescope and a camera designed for simultaneous imaging at four infrared bands, WISE mapped the sky in the mid-infrared at sensitivities over 100 times that of the previous all-sky surveys. The 1024 by 1024 pixel HgCdTe and Si:As detectors on WISE obtained critically sampled images at wavelengths of 3.4, 4.6, 12, and 22 microns (also referred to as W1, W2, W3, and W4, respectively) with spatial resolution ranging from 6 arcseconds at the shortest wavelength to 12 arcseconds at the longest. The two shortest wavelength bands are optimized for the detection of low temperature brown dwarfs and stellar photospheres. The two longer wavelength bands sample the mid-infrared spectral energy distribution of warm dust around star-forming galaxies, asteroids, and stars with circumstellar material. More information on WISE can be found at http://irsa.ipac.caltech.edu/Missions/wise.html.

Terminology

Various sets of WISE image data are available for download. Various multiband catalog source lists are also available, for both the Preliminary and All-Sky release. These are accessible through the IRSA Gator Catalog Tool http://irsa.ipac.caltech.edu/applications/Gator/.

Searching, Results, and Filters

Several search options are provided, which are documented in more detail here. Searching by position is the most popular search.

The search results appear in a table in the top right pane of the search results window. They will include basic information about all the WISE images which fulfill your search criteria. Note that there will be a separate table entry for each WISE band, so the total number of images in the table will be four times the number of WISE framesets or coadds available for the queried region. The listed FITS images can be downloaded by checking the box at the beginning of each row and clicking "Prepare download". (See the page on understanding your search results for more information.)

Filters, either imposed on your initial search or via your results page, can provide a powerful tool for narrowing down search results to the observations you want. For example, you can restrict your search to just one wavelength range by unclicking the other bands. By clicking the "Add Filters" box in the results table pane, it is possible to add criteria to your image search using the tools in the "Filters Dialog" window.

Visualization

The WISE images found by the search are displayed below the search result table. If a single position search was used, this position will be highlighted in a blue box in the center of the field of view. Note that the resolution for WISE band 4 (W4) is half that of W1 (12 versus 6 arcsec), and the coadd resolution is substantially worse than that for single frames in all bands. The position and number of images which fulfill the search are depicted as an overlay on an IRAS 100 micron image in the "Coverage" pane. A second tab in this pane displays a 3 color mosaic of the WISE images.

See the visualization page for more on visualization.

Downloading Data

After deciding which data to download, click on the corresponding checkbox on the left side, and click "Prepare Download" to begin the packaging and data download process -- the download then gets passed to a background job for packaging. See the page on the Background Monitor for more information.
